Informed consent

Investigators: Frederic Font, Panagiota Anastasopoulou

Institution: Universitat Pompeu Fabra (UPF)

Funding body: This project is funded by Music Technology Group of UPF.

Objectives and duration of the project: The goal of this study is to obtain insights about taxonomies and classes for potential applications on the organization of sound collections, such as the Freesound project. Inclusion criteria for participation: This experiment is openly accessible on the internet and its content is suitable for all individuals. However, due to legal reasons, participation is restricted to individuals above the age of 14.

Risks and benefits: Participating in this study does not entail risks greater than those ordinarily encountered in daily life. Additionally, we cannot and do not guarantee that you will receive any benefits from this study.

Privacy: As part of this process, we may collect your IP address to prevent duplicate responses, maintain the quality of the data collected and identify the geographic location of your country for research and analysis purposes. Your IP address will not be used to identify you personally, and we will take appropriate measures (such as storing digital data with access control systems) to protect your personal information from unauthorized access, disclosure, or misuse. By participating in this survey, you agree to the collection and use of your IP address for the purposes described above. According to the aforementioned, in case of data publishing only anonymous data will be distributed.

Voluntary nature of participation: Your participation in this study is on a voluntary basis and you may withdraw from the study at any time without having to justify why.

Contact information: If you have any question about this study, you may contact the PI (Dr. Frederic Font, frederic.font@upf.edu) or the experiment coordinator (Panagiota Anastasopoulou, panagiota.anastasopoulou@upf.edu). If you have doubts, complaints, or questions about this study or about your rights as a research participant, you may contact UPF's Institutional Committee for the Ethical Review of Projects (CIREP) by phone (+34 93 542 21 86) or by email (secretaria.cirep@upf.edu). CIREP is not part of the research team and will treat any information you send confidentially.
